# Texas Hold'em for Kids — Learn Real Poker with Toys!

## Overview
A colorful, gender-neutral **Texas Hold'em** game designed to teach children aged **4–9** all 10 real poker hand rankings, community cards, betting strategy, and decision-making — using toy cars, gems, crowns, and unicorns instead of money. No gambling language, no real currency — just learning wrapped in confetti and bright colors.

### What Kids Learn
- **All 10 hand rankings:** Royal Flush → High Card, taught progressively
- **Community cards:** Flop (3 cards), Turn (4th), River (5th) — the real Hold'em flow
- **Best-hand selection:** Picking the best 5 cards from 7 (2 hole + 5 community)
- **Pattern recognition:** Pairs, flushes, straights, full houses
- **Betting & risk management:** Using toy currency to learn bet/check/fold
- **Reading the board:** What could the opponent have?
- **Position awareness:** Why going last is an advantage
- **Sportsmanship:** Winning gracefully, losing gently ("Dealer wins this time!")

### Toy-Based Currency (Kid-Friendly Metaphors)
Instead of chips or money, kids bet with toys:
- ⭐ Stars (1 toy) — basic currency
- 🚗 Toy Cars (2 toys) — small bets
- 🦄 Unicorns (3 toys) — medium bets
- 💎 Gems (5 toys) — bigger bets
- 👑 Crowns (10 toys) — grand bets

Toy floor of 10 — kids can never go broke.

## Architecture Decisions
- **Single-file component (`App.jsx`):** The entire game lives in one file. This is intentional — the game is small, self-contained, and benefits from locality.
- **Inline styles over CSS:** Keeps animations co-located with components. Global keyframes live in a `<style>` tag inside `App`.
- **No state management library:** `useState` + `useCallback` is sufficient. Do NOT add Redux/Zustand/etc.
- **No router:** Single-screen app. No routing needed.

## Game Rules (Real Texas Hold'em, Simplified for Kids)
- **Deck:** Standard 52 cards, reshuffled each hand
- **Hole cards:** Player and Dealer each get 2 private cards
- **Community cards:** 5 shared cards dealt in stages: Flop (3), Turn (1), River (1)
- **Hand evaluation:** Best 5-card hand from 7 available (2 hole + 5 community), using C(7,5) = 21 combinations
- **All 10 hand rankings:** Royal Flush, Straight Flush, Four of a Kind, Full House, Flush, Straight, Three of a Kind, Two Pair, One Pair, High Card
- **Betting:** Simplified bet/check/fold with toy currency (in levels that teach betting)
- **Blinds:** 1 toy per hand (in betting levels)
- **Showdown:** Remaining players reveal, best hand wins the pot
- **Kicker rules:** Full tiebreaker logic implemented

## Level System: 4 Worlds, 20 Levels

### World 1: "Card Kingdom" (Levels 1–5) — Learn Cards
Flop-only, no betting. Teaches card values, suits, colors, face cards.

### World 2: "Pattern Palace" (Levels 6–10) — Hand Recognition
Adds Turn and River. Teaches pairs, flushes, straights, hand ranking identification.

### World 3: "Toy Market" (Levels 11–15) — Betting with Toys
Full community cards + toy betting. Teaches bet/check/fold, risk/reward, board reading.

### World 4: "Champion Arena" (Levels 16–20) — Full Game
Complete Texas Hold'em. Position awareness, board reading, tournament play.

## Quiz System
- Dynamic generation from actual dealt cards via `generateQuiz(type, hole, community, dealerHole)`
- Types: card-value, suit-color, compare, face-cards, spot-pair, spot-flush, spot-straight, spot-hand, best-hand, bet-decision, risk-reward, read-board, position, mixed-basic, mixed-all
- Correct = ⭐ + toy + encouraging message
- Wrong = gentle "Not quite!" + educational explanation

## Badges (12 total)

## Conventions
- Use `'Fredoka One', cursive` for display text, `Nunito, sans-serif` for body
- Color palette: #FF4757 (red), #FFA502 (orange), #2ED573 (green), #1E90FF (blue), #6c5ce7 (purple), #FFD700 (gold)
- All buttons use the `.btn` class for shared hover/active transitions
- Animations: prefer CSS @keyframes over JS animation libraries
- Touch targets: minimum 44x44px for all interactive elements (Apple HIG)
- Keep everything child-safe: no gambling language, no real money references
- Gender-neutral language and imagery throughout
- Encouraging tone: celebrate wins enthusiastically, soften losses
- Age-appropriate vocabulary: short sentences, familiar words, emoji-heavy visual cues
- Toy metaphors: "toys" not "chips", "pot" shows toy count, "fold" = "sit out"

## Design Philosophy
Kids learn best when they're having fun. Every interaction should feel rewarding:
- Hand rankings taught progressively (not all at once)
- Community cards revealed with animations
- Toy currency makes betting tangible and fun (not scary)
- Quizzes use the actual dealt cards (not canned questions)
- Hand rank badges appear as you build hands
- Confetti and bouncing emojis celebrate wins
- Losses are gentle and immediately offer another round
- Visual hand guide always one tap away
